5 Tips to Double Your Response Rate

Tip 01
Always attach a visual
Never send a cold outreach email without a screenshot of the specific issue. Circle it in red. It proves you visited, makes the problem tangible, and gives them something to forward to their team.
Tip 02
Anchor to lost profit, not design
Store owners don't lie awake thinking about their font size. They lie awake thinking about their conversion rate.
"Your body text is below 16px on mobile, which triggers iOS auto-zoom during checkout — a known cause of cart abandonment."
Tip 03
Use the compliment sandwich
Before presenting problems, find one thing they're doing well. Lead with it. It gives the audit credibility and makes you sound like a partner rather than a critic.
"The product photography on your homepage is excellent — it's clearly doing a lot of work for the brand. That's exactly why I want to make sure the checkout flow is as strong as the top of funnel."
Tip 04
The Loom power move
A 90-second screen recording of you navigating their broken mobile checkout is worth more than any written report. Record it, keep it under 2 minutes, put VIDEO in the subject line. It's the single highest-leverage thing you can do to increase response rates on cold outreach.
Tip 05
Soften the ask
Don't ask for a 60-minute meeting — that sounds like work. Ask for a 10-minute call. Don't ask "are you ready to move forward?" — ask "which of these would you want to tackle first?" Smaller asks get faster yeses from busy people.
